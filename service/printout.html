What are you looking for in a school? What qualities would you say are most important for your child and family?
We are looking for a supportive, creative and nurturing environment for our son to excel at school. We are also looking for heart-centered teachers who take into consideration the whole child. We have no interest in rote learning or teaching to the standards. As a family we are looking for a small community where we can build long-term relationships with other families. Our child loves to learn, enjoys project-based learning, and group activities. Finnegan has enjoyed being in Mexico for a year and would love to stay because of the cultural and ever-changing environment. We are less concerned about what Finnegan is learning and more about how he is taught to learn and inquire about the world around him.
Why are you seeking an alternative to your child's current educational setting?
Finnegan is a sensitive boy and we would not want him to be put into the public school system. We are leaving our year in Mexico and are relocating to Austin for a variety of reasons.
Describe your child's experience with school/homeschooling/unschooling. Does he/she enjoy it? Does he/she have positive interactions with teachers? With peers?
Finnegan loves school and enjoys making friends with other children at school. He only needs a few close friends to feel accepted in his environment. He has liked most of his teachers and is a very well-liked student by his teachers because of his inquisitive nature and his great class participation.
What level/kind of involvement do you have in your child's current educational setting?
We participate in field trips when possible and help with homework when necessary. We have a great relationship with the director of his current school and most of his teachers. There are very few opportunities for volunteering in his current school. While in Canada, I was the Movie Night Coordinator and we were very active in helping to raise money for Finnegan's school. We enjoyed getting to know other parents and stay active with the parents and teachers. We have always helped with extra curricular activities when our schedules permitted.
Parent's Perspective
What are your child's hobbies and special interests?
Finnegan enjoys reading, Legos, and building things. He has just started guitar lessons and is enjoying it, before this he was doing an electronic music class. He enjoys biking, skiing and swimming.
Please describe your child's academic and social strengths
Finnegan is an above average reader, his current teacher feels he reads at about an 8th grade level. He catches on quickly and loves to participate in class. He relates well to children of a variety of ages and skill levels. Finnegan loves doing art projects and building his own tools.
In which areas (both academic and social) do you feel that your child could benefit from the most guidance?
Since he is an only child, he could use help in negotiating when he gets teased by other children and knowing when to walk away from situations that are harmful for him. Finnegan could use help in being more organized and have neater writing and printing. He needs attention with cursive writing.
What are your top three reasons for choosing alternative education for Childhood/Elementary?
1. Move away from the model of teaching to standards 2. We want school to be a creative and interesting atmosphere 3. We want our son to be around other children and families that are open-minded and free thinkers
Are there any other skills or talents your family may be able to contribute?
In Canada I arranged quarterly movie nights and loved putting that together. My husband is also a meditation teacher and could be available for that with the students depending upon his work schedule.
